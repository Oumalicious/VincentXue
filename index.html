Piano for the spirit

Defeat is my success. I'm not afraid of making mistakes because they show me I have something to work on. I'm equally passionate about coding and my hobbies, so this section is dedicated to my two pastimes.

I picked up the piano when I was 8. I applied for Mark Twain Intermediate School but failed the entry tests in both math and music. My piano teacher at the time was extremely dissapointed in me and made my cry in front of my parents. "I have 9 students and 7 of them got into Mark Twain. Vincent is one of the two students who did not get in. He should be ashamed of himself." My parents fired the teacher and I started to only play music I found online. At first, I played piano pieces arranged by youtubers to spite my previous teacher and the ABRSM (Associated Board of the Royal Schools of Music). Over several years, my intial drive to prove someone made me become a better piano player. I eventually continued my path in ABRSM and passed their final Grade 8 exam while I was in highschool. And now, I'm practising pieces that I used to not be able to play.

Running has been my replacement for competitive swimming. It can be done anywhere, but New York City has fantastic running spots like Central Park, Prospect Park, and Shore Road. Flexible joints and posture are key to running. However, I was convinced that my body was physically incapable of flexibility and endurance. I couldn't touch my toes or run more than 2 miles without stopping. My flat feet and bowed legs make long distance running hurt my feet and a doctor I met discouraged me from running. Then I met several runners and personal trainers and they told me tips for me to work on my problems, but it required time and dedication. I learned to have patience and grinded on my physical fit. I was able to touch my toes in less than a week and my posture became ideal in a few months. Initially I couldn't run more than 2 miles, but now I completed the NYC Marathon twice. I also did two 5 Boro Bike Tours and one mini triathalon. These type of activities also taught me that physical strength doesn't mean having a six pack or benching a heavy weight. (almost) Anybody can fit their schedule to include a light run and some stretches once or twice a week. Witnessing overweight and disabled runners pass me in a marathon show me that people should realize their potentials and stop gatekeeping themselves.

My coding journey started at Brooklyn Technical Highschool. In your sophmore year at specialized highschool, you get to pick your 'major' for remaining time of school. Choosing Software Engineering, I learned HTML, CSS, Javascript, and Java. At my time in BTHS, I emulated my childhood point and click adventure games, created a wikipedia page, and developed a lock picking simulator. I got an early understanding of Object Oriented Programming and the SDLC Life Cycle. I continued my interest in Computer Science at Brooklyn College because it was affordable and I worked part-time as a lifeguard.

At the start of 2018, I interned as a FullStack Engineer at the CDPAP agency Allstar Homecare Agency. I was glad to be a part of the CDPAP workforce because Home Health Aides complement the holistic preferences of Chinese culture. Traditional Asian households emphasize the importance of filial piety, so providing a HHA to a family member provides company and a piece of mind. My tasks included display HHA and client information (ReactJS, Node.js, and MongoDB), stylzing with open-sourced frame works (Elemental.UI and AgGrid), and parsing document data (jQuery, Moment.js, and PDFtk).

My proudest project is an exam simulator with an emphasis of memorization by manipulating the DOM and reading JSON files. This app is unique because it's a layered vocabulary memorization tool. There are 3 aspects when it comes to learning character based languages: the character itself (樂/乐), pronunciation (Lè), and the translation (happy). Initially, this was a whimsical project that helped me study for 1 Chinese test, but I saw its potential to be more dynamic. Tests are editable and combinable, can save incorrect answers into a test, and logs the elapsed time per each question. I showed the app to my friend who was pursuing a Master's in Occupational Therapy, and it provided positive results. I see a future where anybody can use my app to study.
