Defeat is my success. I'm not afraid of making mistakes because they show me I have something to work on. I'm equally passionate about coding and my hobbies, so this section is dedicated to my two pastimes.

I picked up the piano when I was 8. I applied for Mark Twain Intermediate School but failed the entry tests in both math and music. My piano teacher at the time was extremely dissapointed in me and made my cry in front of my parents. "I have 9 students and 7 of them got into Mark Twain. Vincent is one of the two students who did not get in. He should be ashamed of himself." My parents fired the teacher and I started to only play music I found online. At first, I played piano pieces arranged by youtubers to spite my previous teacher and the ABRSM (Associated Board of the Royal Schools of Music). Over several years, my intial drive to prove someone made me become a better piano player. I eventually continued my path in ABRSM and passed their final Grade 8 exam while I was in highschool. And now, I'm practising pieces that I used to not be able to play.

Running has been my replacement for competitive swimming. It can be done anywhere, but New York City has fantastic running spots like Central Park and Shore Road. Flexible joints and posture are key to running. However, I was convinced that my body was physically incapable of flexibility and endurance. I couldn't touch my toes or run more than 2 miles without stopping. My flat feet and bowed legs make long distance running hurt my feet. Then I met several runners and they told me ways for me to work on my problems, but it required time and dedication. I learned to have patience and slowly worked on everything. I was able to touch my toes in less than a week and my posture became ideal in a few months. Initially I couldn't run more than 2 miles, but now I completed the NYC Marathon twice. I also did two 5 Boro Bike Tours and one mini triathalon. These type of activities also taught me that physical strength doesn't translate into being a healthy or fit person. Witnessing overweight and disabled runners pass me in a marathon show me that people should realize their potentials and stop gatekeeping themselves.
My coding journey starter at Brooklyn Technical Highschool. In your second year at this school, you get to pick your 'major' for the next two years. I majored in Software Engineering. I learned HTML/CSS/Javascript in my Junior year and Java in my senior year.

In first month of 2018, I had an internship at All Star Home Care Agency in Brooklyn, NY. My job was to improve the company's Web App. I implemented a clocking system, created a pdf extractor with pdftk, and worked on scripts to update the information displayed on the App. For 11 months at my internship, my supervisor taught me the basic ropes of ReactJS and MongoDB.

One of my favorite joys of coding is combining seperate works into the production file. In my Game Design class using Unity, my team and I created a maze game. I worked on the scripts, one member worked on the sprites, and one member worked on the menu/UI. This class taught me the importance of planning and scoping.

I also created an app to study for my Chinese Test. My test questions were initially hard coded and comprised of a three-way match the column mechanism. In my Senior Independant/Group Project class, I polished it into a flexible app that focuses on retesting the incorrect questions by using JSON files.
